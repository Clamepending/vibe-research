## Question

Does banning the baseline's named attractors (Silent opener, abstract closers, specific stock imagery, spring/dawn season-lock) — *without* a positive anchor — produce varied haiku, or does it redirect Sonnet 4.6 to a *different* stereotype attractor (which would reproduce the horror pattern)?

## Hypothesis

**Prior (75% confident):** ban-only shifts Sonnet off the Zen-pastiche attractor but routes it to a *new* stereotype — most likely a "literary-sounding" pastiche (imagist / objectivist / Pound translation) or an over-corrected absurdist / surreal register. Attractor-naming predicts this.
**Falsifier:** ban-only produces varied craft with no detectable new stereotype across n=6. Would mean bans alone work on haiku unlike on horror, weakening the attractor-naming generalization.

## Experiment design

Change: add system prompt with ban list from baseline's observed attractors (Silent opener, abstract closers, cherry blossoms, crow, fog, spring/dawn) plus "output three lines only" meta-guard. No positive instruction.
Measure: (a) do the banned forms still appear, (b) what new attractor (if any) emerges, (c) subjective hit rate, (d) how it compares to baseline.
